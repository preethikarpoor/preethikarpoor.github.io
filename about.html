About Me

Exoplanets, photometry pipelines, and planetary systems — from India to California and back again.

Hello!

I’m Preethi R. Karpoor, an observational astronomer charting the skies for planetary systems beyond our own. My work lies at the intersection of exoplanet demographics, machine learning, and instrumentation-driven science. Currently, I serve as a Research Associate at the Indian Institute of Astrophysics and a Research Affiliate at Louisiana State University, collaborating on projects spanning TESS light curve analysis, Roman mission photometric precision, and atmospheric modeling for Keck/SCALES. I also contribute to the Cool Stars Lab at UC San Diego, where I previously trained under Prof. Adam Burgasser and Prof. Chris Theissen.

Though I began as a Mechanical Engineer, my path shifted toward astrophysics — driven by a fascination with planetary architectures and the origins of habitability. From building scalable pipelines to spectroscopic vetting, I’ve found myself grounded not in methods, but in the questions they serve. As the Bhagavad Gita says: "नभः स्पृशं दीप्तम्" (Touch the sky with glory) — that spirit of ascent, clarity, and discipline shapes my journey through the cosmos.
